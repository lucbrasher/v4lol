What is Sim Racing?

Sim racing (simulation racing) is one of the most immersive motorsport experiences you can have without stepping foot on an actual track. Using high-fidelity physics engines, force feedback steering wheels, and pedal setups, sim racers replicate the feel of driving real race cars as closely as possible. From Formula 1 to NASCAR to endurance racing — if it happens on a real track, you can race it in a sim.

I got into sim racing because of my passion for motorsport and competition. It's not just a game — it's a skill that takes hundreds of hours to master, requiring car control, racecraft, setup knowledge, and mental discipline.

Popular Sim Racing Titles

Not all racing games are created equal. Here's a breakdown of the major sim platforms, ranked by realism:

  1. iRacing — The gold standard for online competitive racing
    • Subscription-based with licensed real-world cars and tracks
    • Massive online community and official league races
    • Used by real NASCAR and IndyCar drivers for training
  2. Assetto Corsa Competizione — Best for GT racing
    • Official GT World Challenge game
    • Incredible tire and aerodynamic physics
    • Stunning graphics and weather simulation
  3. Assetto Corsa — Best modding community
    • Thousands of free community-made cars and tracks
    • Great entry point for beginners
    • Content Manager app greatly enhances the experience
  4. rFactor 2 — Cutting-edge physics engine
    • Preferred by many professional motorsport teams
    • Dynamic track surface that evolves through a race weekend
  5. Gran Turismo 7 — Best console sim
    • PlayStation exclusive with stunning car detail
    • Great for beginners transitioning from casual to sim

Getting Started — Gear Guide

You don't need to spend a fortune to get into sim racing. Here's what you need at each budget level:

  1. Entry Level ($150–$300)
    • Logitech G29 or G920 wheel + pedals
    • Any PC or PlayStation
    • Assetto Corsa or Gran Turismo 7
    • A sturdy desk or basic wheel stand
  2. Mid Range ($500–$1,500)
    • Fanatec CSL DD or Thrustmaster T-GT wheel base
    • Dedicated sim rig frame (Next Level Racing, GT Omega)
    • Load cell brake pedals for better braking feel
    • iRacing or Assetto Corsa Competizione subscription
  3. High End ($2,000+)
    • Direct drive wheel base (Fanatec DD2, Simucube)
    • Full motion platform rig
    • Triple monitor or VR headset setup
    • Hydraulic or air-powered pedals
